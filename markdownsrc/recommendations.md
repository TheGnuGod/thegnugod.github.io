## Video

- [The Best Way To Count](https://youtu.be/rDDaEVcwIJM?si=isviDHIrdpXkw3Dw) is a long introduction (it goes beyond an introduction, but is definitely <em>introductory</em>) to why binary is the best base for humans, and should't just be for computers. It's a great video, but it is largely a response video to the next item on this list, so make sure you watch that first.
- [A Better Way To Count](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qID2B4MK7Y0&t=912s), a short video about why seximal is the best base for human use.

Both of these pose good arguments for either side, but I do think that *The Best Way To Count* is the one that's convinced me that binary is the best base.

- [Do Chairs Exist](https://youtu.be/fXW-QjBsruE?si=4nl9W80d0JHt_36I) is one of my favourite [Vsauce](https://youtube.com/@vsauce) videos ever, and it really dives deep into the questions about what it even means for something to exist. I think this video really challenges very funamental notions about the universe we all hold.

- [Can You Win this One-in-a-Million Game?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmWQmZXYd74&t=34s) or as it's called outside of its YouTube thumbnail, *Rock Paper Scissors* is a completely unique video on the platform of YouTube. Nothing else I've seen is so engaging, interactive and yet perfectly predictable and cheatable in the same way as this. You may not like it and that's not fine. But if you do, you will absolutely love it and I hope you try to explore as far as you can!
I cannot stress enough that **you only get one shot to watch this video honestly** and that you **must play correctly the first time**. Once you've done that, explore the paths and see what you can find, and most definitely do a run where you pretend to win every time (the true ending is amazing), but **play honestly the first time**.
