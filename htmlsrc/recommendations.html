Gnu Recommends

Part of A Thing Gnu Makes

I recommend that you are recommended these recommendations

Disclaimer: This is not a list of all the videos I like, or Podcasts I've listened to, or video games I've played. These are my favourites among favourites, that I think should be watched by anyone who would come to this web page. Also it probably isn't finished.

Video

  • The Best Way To Count is a long introduction (it goes beyond an introduction, but is definitely introductory) to why binary is the best base for humans, and should't just be for computers. It's a great video, but it is largely a response video to the next item on this list, so make sure you watch that first.

    YouTube link to The Best Way To Count

  • A Better Way To Count is a short video about why seximal is the best base for human use.

    YouTube link to A Better Way To Count by Jan Misali

Both of these pose good arguments for either side, but I do think that The Best Way To Count is the one that's convinced me that binary is the best base.

  • Do Chairs Exist? is one of my favourite Vsauce videos ever, and it really dives deep into the questions about what it even means for something to exist. I think this video really challenges very funamental notions about the universe we all hold.

    YouTube link to Do Chairs Exist by Vsauce

  • Can You Win This One-in-a-Million Game? or as it's called outside of its YouTube thumbnail, Rock Paper Scissors is a completely unique video on the platform of YouTube. Nothing else I've seen is so engaging, interactive and yet perfectly predictable and cheatable in the same way as this. You may not like it and that's not fine. But if you do, you will absolutely love it and I hope you try to explore as far as you can! I cannot stress enough that you only get one shot to watch this video honestly and that you must play correctly the first time. Once you've done that, explore the paths and see what you can find, and most definitely do a run where you pretend to win every time (the true ending is amazing), but play honestly the first time.

    YouTube link to Can You Win this One-in-a-Million Game? by CGP Grey

  • The Unbreakable Krypros Code is a video about a piece of crytographic art that hasn't been solved by the greatest cryptographers of our time and the one before. It has beautiful editing, clean explanations and strikes a great balance of not telling you everything ever about the topic but also not depriving you of information.

    YouTube link to The Unbreakable Kryptos Code by LEMMiNO

Podcasts

  • Cortex is a podcast where CGP Grey and Myke Hurley discuss their working lives. The two contentent creators across two different mediums talk about how they work and interact with technology and the world to help them be productive.

    Link to Cortex's page on the Relay website and its RSS Feed

  • In Robot or Not? Jason Snell asks John Siracusa to deicide on exactly what even slightly vague words mean. The podcast originally asked whether a given thing is a (you guessed it) robot, or not. Now they discuss everything from whether a mushroom tea is soup, if ice is food or what it means for someone to be a "Friend of the Show". As soon as a new episode comes out, I always rush to listen to it as soon as I can, postponing whatever (now pointless) thing I was doing before.

    Link to Robot or Not on The Incomparable and its RSS Feed

Games

  • Hollow Knight is the genre-reviving 2017 metroidvania video game developed and published by Team Cherry. It has beautiful art, great design and engaging mechanics and constantly feels like you're facing the perfect level of challenge to feel definitively difficult but still doable and rewarding.

    Link to the Hollow Knight webpage and Steam store page
