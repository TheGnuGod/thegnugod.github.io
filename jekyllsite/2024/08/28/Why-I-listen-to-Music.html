Why I listen to Music

Post #1 on my blog, published 28 Aug 2024

Music isn’t the way to get smarter. It’s not the way to get caught up on the new news. When you listen to music on your own, why do it? You can hear music by playing a video game, and then you get to play the video game too! What’s the point?

I will say upfront that music can be enjoyed in many ways, and most of them aren’t the focus of this article. You can listen to music in different places, often it’ll be in the background and sometimes you’re with your friends, or at any number of special occasions. Here, music is obviously the choice, but when you’re on your own, listening critically, just to the song… it isn’t so clear. You could listen to a podcast instead, hear your favourite hosts and learn new things. Or watch television, read a book, and even doomscroll for hours, then realise you’ve wasted your life and cry! (maybe that last one isn’t the move)

But there’s one thing that music gets me that nothing else does. It’s that “HELL YEAH , THAT WAS FRICKIN’ AWESOME” feeling that a good song gives you (especially on a first listen).

Case in point: Thicc, by sungazer

The two main points where I get that feeling are: where the “my vocal cords are feeling a little thicc and heavy” lyric repeats for the second time, and the song begins for real; as well as when that piano comes in during the crazy synth part. Both of these just feel so good and I just have to bop to them, with the biggest smile on my face. You would not believe how happy I get when I hear good music. It is amazing how desperate I am to try and get other people to hear to what I heard and feel it too.

No other medium gives me this feeling.

This is the music drug that keeps me coming back. This is the reason music is worth talking about. This is why I listen to music.
